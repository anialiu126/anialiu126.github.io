## Gliding

When I am not chasing gravitational waves, you will often find me chasing
thermals. I am passionate about **gliding** — flying engine-less sailplanes,
kept aloft only by rising air, with nothing but the quiet of the sky around you.

I learned to fly with the **Oxford University Gliding Club**, where I went
**solo** — still one of the proudest moments of my life. I later served as the
club's **Secretary**, helping to keep our little community of pilots organised
and in the air.

Some of my favourite flying has been in the mountains. On a club expedition to
**La Motte, in the French Alps**, I got to soar along ridgelines and ride
Alpine thermals high above the peaks — an unforgettable way to see a landscape.

Gliding and astrophysics turn out to share something for me: both reward
patience, a feel for invisible forces, and a love of the long view. Reading a
thermal is not so different from reading a signal — you learn to trust the
patterns in something you cannot see.
